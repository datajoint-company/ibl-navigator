International Brain Laboratory Behavioral Data Portal

The International Brain Laboratory is a team of 22 experimentalists and theorists, working collaboratively to understand the computations that support decision-making in the brain.

This portal contains behavioral data from a standardized training pipeline, implemented in 9 labs of 7 institutions worldwide. Mice learn to make decisions that combine incoming visual evidence with internal beliefs about the dynamic structure of the environment. Through this portal, users can view behavioral data from mice throughout their training, and see the transition from novice to expert behavior unfold.

The content of this portal reflects a sub-selected 103 mice out of over 600 mice studied in the project as of 11/30/2019. 101 of the mice were used in the publication "A standardized and reproducible method to measure decision-making in mice".

Behavioral Paradigm

The IBL behavioral data is generated during a dynamic foraging task for mice1. Mice are trained to judge the spatial location of a visual stimulus (vertical grating) and report their decision by moving a wheel to the left or right2. The contrast of the visual stimulus can be varied so that decisions to range from easy to ambiguous. This allows quantification of the animals' threshold, bias, and lapse rate (below). Data are collected daily in 9 labs of 7 research institutions across three countries, during both a training and an expert phase. For trained mice, the reward probability for each choice option can be experimentally manipulated, encouraging mice to “forage” among different choice alternatives. Behavior during decision-making is characterized both using traditional metrics, such as choice and reaction time, along with video recordings from 3 high-speed cameras.

Training Criteria

The animal was habituated for 3 days and trained started after that. The animal is identified as trained based on the following criteria:

  1. 0% and 6% contrasts had been introduced to the contrast set, 2.
  2. >200 trials were completed with >80% performance on easy (100% and 50% contrasts) trials in each of the last three sessions
  3. A four-parameter psychometric curve (bias, lapse left, lapse right, threshold) fitted to performance on all trials from the last three sessions had parameter values of bias < 16, threshold < 19, and lapses < 0.2.

If the animal has been trained for over 40 days but still did not achieve the training status, the status will be marked as “over40days”.
